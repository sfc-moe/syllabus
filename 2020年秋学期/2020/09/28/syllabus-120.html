研究会概要

目的・内容

The participants of the seminar firstly understand the process by which start-ups emerge, grow, and internationalise, and secondly develop a case study material as a result of the learning. This enable the participants to accumulate the knowledge required to undertake the management of business and the public to have an access to the latest reality of the start-up ecosystem. The definition of start-up here is very broad, including non-profit, international organization, volunteer organisation, and intra-firm startups. The seminar investigates the process by which such start-ups gradually grow and provide the value to the society.

評価方法

Participation to the discussions, and the quality of final output

課題

The application procedures to be sent upon request. Please contact any of the current members of IBER-Kotosaka B2 seminar.

来期の研究プロジェクトのテーマ予定

Each member to develop any one of the following output per year: A business plan for the organization the one leads A business case study material A research report (e.g., of a industry, technology, or mgt technique) Academic article (can take more than a year if aiming for publication) The output of the individual project is expected to be published in a public output, including but not limited to journals, magazines, and seminar website.

その他・留意事項

The instructor has strong expertise and preference on qualitative research method. The participants should utilise office hours and emails to interact with the instructor. Off-the-class learning will be a core of the learning of this project research seminar; therefore the instructor is ready to answer any relevant and necessary questions or requests even out side of the class. In addition, if any of the participants can not command Japanese, the class will not use any books in Japanese.

授業スケジュール

In each ordinal session, we deal with either case study discussion, journal article reading, or discussion of current topics (including small projects); while, the individual project to be presented and discussed in the two overnight sessions If any good opportunity to come, external collaboration activities (e.g., Konica Minolta, YJ Capital) Apart from the 10 ordinal sessions and 2 overnight sessions per term, we plan two study camps per year, one is the Mid-year Camp and the other is the Year-end Camp. The event is for mid-term presentation and final presentation of individual project Both events to be hosting the Alumni (including the graduates from Ritsumeikan and other universities where some members graduated from)
