研究会概要

目的・内容

The aim of this course is to familiarize students with theoretical and practical issues involved in second language testing, with a focus on the broader concepts and research in the field of assessment. Activities and discussions are centered around giving students hands-on experience with a designing, analyzing, and revising assessments for use in the language classroom. Topics covered in the course include validity, test uses & formats, design & analysis, score reliability, and fairness & ethics in assessment.

While this is a research-oriented seminar, students will engage in both theoretical and practical (i.e., classroom-based) examinations of the uses and interpretations of assessments for second language learning. The course will be a mix of lecture, online interactions, and task-based learning. This class is primarily designed for those who have an interest in language education and research.

This course will survey second and foreign language teaching online methods, approaches, and research for those interested in language teaching & learning. We will look into several major topics in the field of online language teaching, including approaches to teaching different skills, language curriculum, and testing. The goal of the class is to examine language in use and the development of materials, assessments, and learning environments for teaching and research purposes, primarily for English as a foreign, international, or second language purposes.

The course will be a mix of lecture, online interaction, and task-based learning, designed to introduce new ideas, discuss and think through concepts from a contextualized and student-based perspectives, and engage in content through hands-on activities. This class is primarily designed for those who have an interest in language education and research.

By the end of the course, students will be able to: - Understand the basic history of language teaching and related theories - Design materials, tasks, and assessments for language learning - Evaluate the strengths and weaknesses of different approaches and methods to second language teaching - Read, discuss, and evaluate key theories and research related to language education, curriculum design, and assessment
