国際開発論では、国連のミレニアム開発目標のうち、特に、人的資本開発にかかる当ピックを中心に取り上げ、議論する。具体的には、初等教育の普及、女性の地位向上、幼児死亡率や就学前児童の健康などについて、今後の開発政策の方向性を考えるうえで、必要な知識や手法を身に着けることを目標とする。

授業シラバス

主題と目標／授業の手法など

This course is designed to assist students to explore various topics underlying international development from both theoretical and methodological perspectives. We particularly focus on the human capital accumulation which is regarded as a driving force of economic development. More specifically, among the Millennium Development Goals (MDGs) proposed by the United Nations, we will discuss (i) universal education, (ii) gender equality, and (iii) child/maternal health. At the end of the course, students are expected to answer the following specific questions (*) and then propose policy recommendations that decision-makers might utilize in order to optimize certain goals, given budgetary and other constraints. Students will become knowledgeable about economic/statistical analyses of human capital development.

(*) The specific questions that students are expected to answer are: (a) why does "universal education" bring economic development?; (b) why is gender equality in education really important?; (c) Are children's education more effective than adult education?; (d) Does money matters for education? (e) Does maternal health affect children's outcomes later on in their life?

授業計画

第1回 Overview of Poverty in the Developing World

Overview of Poverty in the Developing World

第2回 The data analysis for development

Learn the basics of the regression analysis by using the dataset in developing countries

第3回 Mid-term exam

Mid-term exam

第4回 Why is human capital so important?

This sesson is aimed to learn why human capital investments are playing important role for macroeconomic growth by referring the endogenous growth theory in economics.

第5回 Where has all education gone?

This session will see the contradictory view of the endogenous growth theory - the empirical studies that shows no strong and rigorous associations b/w educational investments in the country and economic growth.

第6回 Why is primary education more important for development?

We will learn the basic idea of the rate of return to education.

第7回 Gender inequality in education

The session will consider the problem of a significant difference in education between boys and girls in developing world.

第8回 Does money matters in education?

We will discuss the so-called "money matters debate" in education.

第9回 The cost-effectiveness approach

We will learn the basic of the cost-effectiveness analysis in education.

第10回 The conditional cash transfer

We will learn the "conditional cash transfer", a silver bullet of poverty reduction, and its role to boost the student enrollments in debeloping countries.

第11回 Randomized controlled trail

The session provide the basic knowledge about the impact evaluation - how to assess the causal impact of a certain set of policies in developing coutries.

第12回 Why is RCT effective for policy making world?

The session will show a wide variety of example of RCTs by demonstraing the research results drawn from economists of the Poverty Action Lab.
