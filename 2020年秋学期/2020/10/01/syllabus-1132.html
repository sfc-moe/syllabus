科目概要

This is a course on U.S. foreign policy with a specific focus on domestic factors which shapes and influences U.S. foreign policy making process. Despite the popular argument of U.S. retreat and decline, U.S. still remains the world's preeminent power. However, it is difficult to deny that U.S. is increasingly becoming an "exhausted power." The American public has become inward-looking as a result of a decade of excess intervention in the 2000s. The polarization of politics is having a negative effect on U.S. presence on the world stage. Budgetary constraint is limiting the overseas deployment of U.S. forces. The course will try to factor in the domestic constraints in foreign policy making as well as highlight the dynamics of interest group politics that shape foreign policy environment in the U.S.

授業シラバス

主題と目標／授業の手法など

Lecture, textbook, and term paper are all in English. Students are required to acquire basic facts and historical knowledge about U.S. foreign policy by reading such text as, Joyce P. Kaufman, A Concise History of U.S. Foreign Policy 3rd ed. (Lanham: Roman & Littlefield, 2014). This is not a course on U.S. diplomatic history. Rather, it will focus on inner dynamics of contemporary U.S. foreign policy making.

教材・参考文献

Steven W. Hook, U.S. Foreign Policy: Paradox of World Power, 6th ed. (Washington DC: CQ Press, 2020).

第5回 Executive Branch

Is the US President “most powerful man” on the planet?

第6回 Congressional Role

Congress has a constitutionally defined foreign policy role.
100 members in the senate and 435 members in the House.
535 voices altogether.
Is it too Chaotic?

第7回 Foreign Policy Bureaucracy

What are the role of bureaucrats?
What do diplomats do?

第8回 Public Opinion

Does public opinion enlighten US foreign policy or is it a distraction?

第9回 Role of Mass Media

Do media enlighten or emflame?
The role of tribal media in politics.

第10回 Social Movements and Interest Groups

You’ve all heard about Indian lobby, Jewish lobby, Taiwan lobby, and others.
There are also issue-oriented interest groups.
How powerful are they?
Do they get to the shape the policy?

第11回 National Security and Defense Policy

America today is a “National Security State.”
Its reach encompass the globe.
How are those missions defined?

第12回 Economic Statecraft & Transnational Policy Issues

Sanctions and other economic state crafts have become an often used tool in foreign policy.
Is it effective?

Many of the issues US faces are transnational issues in nature which it cannot solve alone.
How is US doing on that front?
