研究会概要

目的・内容

This seminar aims to explore and examine our intercultural communication from a critical perspective.

Culture, a widely used but perhaps little understood term, is in fact influenced by economic and political issues which create unequal relations of power between peoples. Since dominant discourses often lack comprehensive understanding of the politics and power relations behind culture and language, a hegemonic relationship can emerge between people who engage in intercultural communication as a result of cultural differences being over- or under-emphasized. Therefore, it is imperative that we understand the politics at work in an intercultural context and develop critical literacy.

We will examine intercultural communication throughout reading and discussing a book or some articles.

Students will also conduct a research on related topics to assigned literature or graduate thesis.

評価方法

On the basis of participation in the seminars, students will be evaluated upon 1) reading session; contribution to the discussion, presentation, and 2) research; a research progress presentation, contribution to the discussion, a term paper and a final presentation.

教材・参考文献

Ingrid Piller, 2017, Intercultural Communication ‐A Critical Introduction, Edinburgh University press.

Satoshi Toyosaki, and Shinsuke Eguchi, 2017, Intercultural communication in Japan : theorizing homogenizing discourse, Routledge.
