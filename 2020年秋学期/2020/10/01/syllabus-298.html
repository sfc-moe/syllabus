科目概要

In 2020. MANAGEMENT OF EMERGING BUSINESS renews the syllabus to explore new businesses to tackle with unpredictable environmental change caused by COVID-19. This class aims at providing students with basic understanding on startup business, such as idea generation, patenting, business modeling, prototyping, commercialization, and incorporation.

授業シラバス

主題と目標／授業の手法など

Firstly, interactions with the guest speakers from different industries and countries enable students to understand the know-how to be a successful entrepreneur. The guest speakers in 2019 are:Dr. David Farber, The internet hall of fame, Dr. Sureswaran Ramadass, Founder of Mlabs, Malaysia, Mr. Hiroki Mashita, CEO of V-CUBE, Startup from Keio University, Mr. Paul Ford, KPMG FAS Co. Ltd, Canada, and Dr. John Choe, CEO & Founder, Encored, Inc., Korea.
Secondly, to empower design-thinking skills and decision-making experiences, students participate in-class exercises, using design thinking methodology, an idea exchange workshop, and case studies in line with IoT & Energy, IoT and Medical, and distance learning. For example, case study discussions help the students have a capability of finding and solving the problem in daily management of a business by place students in the role of the decision-maker.

教材・参考文献

Lecture materials are delivered online.

提出課題・試験・成績評価の方法など

Class evaluation is based on (1) a business plan at the end of the semester, (2) in-class presentation, (3) class participations, and (4) assignments. The content of a business plan follows the Business Plan Template that students take in this class.

授業計画

第1回 Overview and Introduction

Studying and researching in university is the enabler of invention which is convertible to a big business. Our alumni known as a techno-entrepreneur has given proof of it. To be successful entrepreneur, you need to have the special mindset and some essential know-hows to commercialize study in university. Before planning a business and financing seed money, knowing entrepreneurial career is essential. In this lecture, we will discuss the motivation of launching a startup company.

Jiro Kokuryo, Masaki Umejima

第2回 Idea generation for developing a business through the design thinking workshop

Idea generation is the first step to be a successful technology entrepreneur. In this lecture, the students exercise the process of making a business to be a solution to demands in a market through the design thinking workshop.

Jiro Kokuryo, Masaki Umejima

第3回 Idea generation for developing a business through the discussion with the expert

The successful experience tells us that a good idea blushed through the discussion with a specialist who knows the past and explores the future in each field. David Farber, the Internet Hall of Fame and the former Chief Technologist for Federal Communications Commission (FCC) in .U.S., is the man who knows the internet.
In inviting Dr. Farber, we discuss ideas to bring innovation to the internet society.
Guest lecturer, Dr. David Farber, Co-Director, Cyber Civilization Research Center, Keio University

Jiro Kokuryo, Masaki Umejima

第4回 Idea exchange for making distance learning more ideal

In December 1997, Japanese universities started distance learning in response to the report “Handling of distance learning according to university establishment standards” in University Education Council of Ministry of Education, Culture, Sports, Science, and Technology (MEXT). In 1996, one year prior to the government the de-regulation, Keio University, led by Prof. Jun Murai (the internet hall of fame), launched the academic alliance for running SOI Asia in which higher educational institutions jointly operated the high speed internet backed by the satellite telecommunication, working together with 28 leading universities in 14 Asian countries. Since then, universities have fostered know-hows backed by the successful and failure practices. Adopting the internet for communication has allowed more exchanges on course contents among teachers in different universities. In encountering covid-19 crisis, there is a growing need for distance learning. If the user experiences take the leadership of making a new business, now is the best timing to launch the new idea in distance learning. Let’s bury the gaps between an idea and prototype at the workshop.

Jiro Kokuryo, Masaki Umejima

第5回 Learning essential components of making a business -1-

A good business plan guides you through each stage of starting and managing your business. You’ll use your business plan as a roadmap for how to structure, run, and grow your new business. It’s a way to think through the key elements of your business. This lecture explains the structure of a business plan by using the business plan template and the financial booklet. In highlighting 11 essential components on business planning, we learn about the essential knowledge of financing for incorporation.

Jiro Kokuryo, Masaki Umejima

第6回 Business development case, based on university research

We learn about the strategy whereby make a research outcome incorporated? Thus, we will invite a university startup with successful experience to our lecture and discuss about a timeline from idea generation to commercialization.
Prof. Sures is a professor, International University Malaya Wales (IUMW) and a founder of Mlabs. In 1992, he started the basic research on multipoint-to-multipoint video conference technology and successfully commercialized his invention. Since 2005, his company has been publicly listed at Malaysia Stock Exchange.
Guest speaker, Prof.Sureswaran Ramadass, International University Malaya Wales (IUMW), and Chairman of the Board, NLTVC Sdn Bhd

Jiro Kokuryo, Masaki Umejima

第7回 Financing skill

When you shape the management team and write up the business plan, you will need to fundraise to realize your idea and research outcome into society. Venture Capital [VC] is one of the important cash resources for all small companies. But many suggestions by entrepreneurs are rejected. Is there any condition to receive funding? We will invite Venture Capital or the specialist in this sector to argue on what is an attractive business plan for VC.
Guest speaker is Mr. Paul Ford, Director, Transaction Services,KPMG FAS Co. Ltd.

Jiro Kokuryo, Masaki Umejima

第8回 Find the Know-How from a successful startup -1-

We learn about a successful experience to convert university research to a business.
In 1998, Mr. Mashita launched WEB design service, targeted a business customer.
Three years later, with receiving equity money by Keio University, Mr. Mashita incorporated V-CUBE to internationally provide with a WEB meeting service. Since 2015, V-CUBE has been publicly listed at TSE, which is the biggest stock exchange in Japan. Mr. Mashita discuss about know-how to make a startup.
Guest speaker, Mr. Hiroki Mashita, CEO of V-CUBE

Jiro Kokuryo, Masaki Umejima

第9回 Find the Know-How from a successful startup-2-

Softbank, Japanese mobile operator, has formed up a startup fund exceeded $93 Billion in highlighting IOT. More than $93 billion has been secured from backers such as the Saudi Arabia’s Public Investment Fund, Abu Dhabi’s Mubadala Investment Co., Apple Inc., Qualcomm Inc., Foxconn Technology Group and Sharp Corp.
In July 2017, SoftBank announced to invest in Encored, which plays the role of IoT platformer in energy management service in U.S. In inviting the key-persons in that deal, we discuss about their future vision and the purpose of the alliance.
Mr. John Choe, CEO & Founder, Encored, Inc.

Jiro Kokuryo, Masaki Umejima

第10回 How does a startup challenge a big company?

For many years, Japanese electricity business is monopolized in each religion: Tokyo electric company for Tokyo and Kyushu electric company for Kyushu. The de-regulation in 2015 which was motivated by the biggest earth quake in Japan’s 1000 years history became the enabler of creating the new entries.
In this lecture by using a case study, we learn how a startup tackle a big company in the timing when new regulation is valid.

Jiro Kokuryo, Masaki Umejima

第11回 Know the ideal environment to foster a startup

AI, IoT, distance learning, distributed energy resource management, and inhouse medical service. In SFC, there are good cases to know the real of these buzzwords. Plus, in response to the social demand, SFC has undertaken the task to create a startup by providing researchers and students with fluent supports: intellectual property management, business incubation, and so on.

Jiro Kokuryo, Masaki Umejima
